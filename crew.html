Hi, I am Laura
My project investigates how metabolic cues that activate AMPK – a key energy sensor protein – can regulate the membrane traffic of cell surface proteins. In doing this, I have discovered a novel signaling pathway that controls cell migration by regulating the clathrin-mediated endocytosis of 1-integrin. Besides this research, I’m passionate about teaching and mentorship in higher education. Outside of the lab, I enjoy keeping up with reality TV, baking, and walking Toronto’s trails while listening to true crime podcasts.

Hi, I am Shafi
Shafi is currently a second year MSc student in the Molecular Science program at Toronto Metropolitan University. He joined the Antonescu lab in Fall 2022 and his project focuses on how cancer cells adapt to low nutrient environments. Specifically, he studies how triple-negative breast cancer cells can sense and adapt to amino acid availability through the activation of protein recycling pathways and the regulation of glycogen synthase kinase 3 beta. Outside of the lab, Shafi enjoys spending time with family and playing/watching sports with friends.
Hi, I am Cristina
My name is Cristina Camardi and I am a first year M.Sc. student at the Toronto Metropolitan University. I completed my B.Sc. with honours at the University of Guelph majoring in Molecular Biology and Genetics. I am passionate about learning the fundamentals of intracellular signal transduction events and how their regulation influences cellular function and physiology. My project will focus on this very subject, specifically, phosphoinositide (PIP) lipid signaling. Moreover, how acyltransferase LCLAT1 maintains specific PIP species levels, which in turn, will subsequently affect cellular growth, survival and migration. This is particularly relevant in triple-negative breast cancer, as mutations hijacking PIP involving signaling pathways contribute to tumorigenesis. Beyond my research interests, I enjoy walking in nature and practicing yoga.

Hi, I am Natalie
My name is Natalie Uzynski and I am a first year MSc. student at Toronto Metropolitan University (TMU). I completed my HBSc. at the University of Toronto, majoring in Fundamental Genetics as well Cell and Molecular Biology. I then worked in clinical cancer research prior to joining the Molecular Science program at TMU. I am interested in understanding how the disruption of fundamental cellular processes contributes to cancer progression. My project aims to determine how clathrin regulates PI3K-Akt signaling, a pathway upregulated in many cancers. Using cutting-edge microscopy methods, I aim to obtain unprecedented resolution of clathrin structures within cells, and determine the localization of key signal activators and inhibitors. Apart from science, I enjoy matcha, weightlifting, and travelling.
